**Jonathan Blow - Preventing the Collapse of Civilization**

<YTPlayer url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pW-SOdj4Kkk" />

Technological progress is not a given. This one made me want to learn compilers and low-level languages.

<Spacer />

**Bret Victor - Inventing on Principle**

<YTPlayer url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EGqwXt90ZqA" />

On the importance of an immediate connection with whatever it is you're creating. I think about these examples often.

<Spacer />

**Rich Hickey - Hammock-Driven Development**

<YTPlayer url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f84n5oFoZBc" />

Sometimes the best thing is to walk away from the computer. An excellent overview on focused and diffuse modes of thinking.

<Spacer />

**Liz Rice - Containers From Scratch**

<YTPlayer url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8fi7uSYlOdc" />

By far the best explanation I've seen on how containers work, and one of the most insightful videos on Linux in general too. I don't even know Go, didn't matter at all.

<Spacer />
**Cory House - The 7 Pillar Developer**

<YTPlayer url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Nks_Fb5TUjs" />

Some very actionable and well-articulated principles on how to approach a career as a developer.
